The estimator sees the effect.
The default test never fires.

This is the power simulation behind the paper on recreational cannabis legalization and state-level alcohol sales, replayed under three rejection rules: the package default (a wild bootstrap, what summary() returns as conventionally run), a jackknife on the same fits, and a calibrated randomization test. The one control is the true substitution effect δ. Move it and watch which tests fire.

    Notes

    Every number replays Results/csv/power_*.csv and intime_placebo_*.csv, the same files the paper's tables are built from. No estimation runs in the browser. The plausible-effect band marks the low single digits, 1 to 4 percent. The paper treats this as the relevant small-effect case rather than a point estimate (Subbaraman 2016; the 12 percent scanner-data decline of Baggio, Chong and Kwon 2020 is the contrasting high end).

    The paper reports the three-rule comparison in brief (appendix “Inference Rules for the Primary Estimator”, Figure C1); this page replays the full simulation behind it, draw by draw.
